## Teaching & Supervision

I teach information systems, data, and analytics with a practice-oriented approach that connects core concepts to real organisational contexts. I am keen to work with emerging technologies in the classroom: I treat generative AI both as a tool students learn to use well and as something they learn to evaluate critically, and I design authentic, application-based assessment around it. I pick up new tools quickly and bring them into teaching as the field moves.

### Courses taught and developed
- **Introduction to Data and Database Management** — data modelling, SQL, and database administration
- **Introduction to Web Technologies** — HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and responsive design
- **Programming with Python** — problem-solving, version control, and reproducible workflows
- **Data analytics and research methods** — from data preparation and modelling to interpretation
- **Data ethics and AI in society** — privacy, accountability, and the responsible use of AI

### Approach
- Practice-oriented and project-based, drawing on real datasets and industry cases
- Authentic assessment that rewards judgement and reproducible work over generated text
- Open-source-first tools (Python, R) so students build transferable, inspectable skills

### Supervision and effectiveness
- Supervised MSc students through full research projects, from design to analysis and writing
- Student evaluations of approximately 4.6–4.92 out of 5 across seven semesters as sole instructor
- Committed to ongoing pedagogic development, including formal higher-education teaching accreditation (e.g. FHEA)
