## About

I am an information systems researcher and educator working on how organisations adopt, govern, and are reshaped by AI and digital technologies. My work is sociotechnical: I study the interaction between technology, work, and the people and organisations affected by it, and I combine quantitative, qualitative, and computational methods to do so.

A central thread of my current research is **AI in organisations** — algorithmic management, the adoption of generative AI, and the organisational and equity consequences that follow. This sits alongside continuing work on data governance, public-sector data, and cybersecurity, and is grounded in nine years of industry experience managing information systems and technology projects. I am comfortable working with data, from predictive modelling and natural language processing to survey analysis and visualisation, and I am keen to bring emerging technologies into the classroom rather than treat them as a threat to it.

## Research programme

- **AI and algorithmic management in organisations** — how AI-based management systems change autonomy, power, and the employment relationship
- **Generative AI adoption and workplace inequality** — including a working paper on the gender gap in generative AI use among public-sector workers across Europe
- **Digital transformation and data governance** — public-sector digitalisation, open government data, and the design and use of data
- **Cybersecurity and data protection** — machine-learning prediction of data breaches and the security of integrated health-record systems
- **Applied data analytics** — predictive modelling, computational text analysis, and visualisation for decision support
